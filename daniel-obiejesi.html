About Daniel

Daniel Obiejesi is a self-taught visual artist from Lagos, Nigeria. Drawn to art since his childhood, he enjoyed recreating images he saw while watching Japanese anime and documentaries on ancient civilisations. In 2020, Daniel began creating using a digital medium to enable him to create art on the go while travelling for work. He often shared his work online and enlisted in contests, eventually winning 1st place in the COLDS - Holaplex Digital Art Contest. Daniel is influenced by artists like James Jean. The unfolding of each art work offers Daniel a space for connecting with himself.

Artists Statement: The human experience is vibrant and mysterious. Through my artwork I channel both of these energies from my experiences and reflections on, especially from a metaphysical perspective. I often start creating from a black canvas because I find this void more inviting to fill with my ideas. My art incorporates symbology and sacred geometry (languages underpinning reality) from across different cultures and eras to evoke the cosmic heritage within us all. I am primarily influenced by the genre of visionary art, exploring themes around consciousness and all things mystical.
